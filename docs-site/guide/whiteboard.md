# Whiteboard & Ideas

The Whiteboard is a sticky-note overlay for quick brainstorming and idea tracking. It floats on top of your canvas and is separate from your diagram content.

## Opening the Whiteboard

- Press **`Ctrl+I`** (I for Ideas) to toggle the whiteboard, or
- Click the **Whiteboard** button in the toolbar

The whiteboard appears as a full-screen overlay with a semi-transparent backdrop and grid pattern.

## Working with Sticky Notes

### Adding Notes

Click the **Add Note** button in the whiteboard to create a new sticky note.

### Editing Notes

Click on a note to start typing. Notes support basic formatting:

- **`Ctrl+B`** for bold
- **`Ctrl+I`** for italic

### Moving Notes

**Drag** any note to reposition it on the whiteboard.

### Resizing Notes

**Drag** the corner handles of a note to resize it.

### Coloring Notes

Each note has a **color picker** — click it to choose from 12 preset colors plus any recent colors from your palette. The note header and text automatically adjust contrast for readability.

### Deleting Notes

Click the **delete button** (×) on a note to remove it.

## Closing the Whiteboard

- Press **`Escape`** or click outside the whiteboard area
- Press **`Ctrl+I`** again
- Click the **close button**

## How It's Saved

Whiteboard notes are saved with your document automatically. They persist across sessions, so your ideas are always there when you come back.

## Export Behavior

By default, whiteboard notes are **excluded** from exports (PNG, SVG, PDF). The whiteboard is designed for drafting and brainstorming, not for final output.

There's an **"Include whiteboard notes"** option in the export dialog if you want to include them.

## Tips

- **Brain dump first** — use the whiteboard to capture ideas quickly before committing to diagram structure
- **Color-code by category** — use different colors for different concerns (e.g., yellow for questions, green for decisions, red for blockers)
- **Keep it messy** — the whiteboard is your scratchpad, not your final diagram
